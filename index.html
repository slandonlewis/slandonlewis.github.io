About Me

I am a junior software developer who got his certification from Nashville Software School at the age of 20. The world of technology has always been a big part of my life. At age 9, I discovered a programming platform for children known as Scratch. Little did I know my life would be forever changed and my love for the code would ignite here! For several years, I would be the oddball in my friend group who spent all of his time building projects.

During my last few years of high school, I was introduced to the world of JavaScript. I went through many YouTube playlists, freeCodeCamp and CodeCademy lessons, and even Scrimba courses to get as much practice as I possibly could with the language. The more I learned, the hungrier and more excited I became for greater knowledge and progress!

Many years later, this craving for knowledge would lead me to Nashville Software School, where I attended the Full Time Web Developer Bootcamp. This 6 month program taught everything from basic data structures, iteration, functions, and debugging to concepts such as using and building Web APIs, working with different programming languages as well as their frameworks and libraries, Object Oriented Programming, and SO much more! But most importantly, NSS taught us the importance of teamwork through planning and building a number of group-based projects. All of the solo studying I tackled prior to that course could never amount to the experience I gained from the hands-on, team building exercises and supportive environment.

I owe everything to my former instructors and classmates from NSS, and the tech community as a whole, for getting me as far as I have gotten. That's why I plan to give it all back by using what I've learned to contribute to the growth of companies and businesses in need of developers like me!

If you are curious about my story and experience at NSS, check out my interview from the Stories From The Hackery Podcast!
